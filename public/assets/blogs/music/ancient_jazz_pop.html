Make Some Mention

0:00 / 0:00

This song was a ton of fun to write. The synth and background vocals really have a rhythm of their own. The drums and orchestra forces them into a 4/4 grid. I remember being obsessed with the tuba and really inspired by Musicians from New Orleans.

The vocalist on this track is Lawrence Lee. The studio had just purchased one of the first microphones Monheim Microphones produced. We decided to test drive the mic on this track. Lawrence laid amazing vocals and shortly before finishing we noticed the mics noise floor was slowly getting louder. Andrew Monheim took the mic, did some repairs and quickly returned it to us. We then begane recording vocals again, and again near the end of the session the noise floor was getting loud. Andrew rushed to get us taken care of and this time nailed the repair. Lawrence Lee graciously came back a 3rd time and finished the track. I will forever appreciate Larence's patience and Andrew's quick care.

The track was mixed and mastered by Tim Webberson. One of my best friends and a brilliant mixing engineer.

Paris Rain

0:00 / 0:00

When this song was first written it sampled a scene from Sabrina with Humphrey Bogart and Audrey Hepburn. We loved the vibe but wanted some adjustments so we rewrote the scene and re-recorded it.

I asked Mae the Pirate to help me write lyrics and sing on the track. While we were writing I stepped out for a seccond. When I returned I saw that Mae had deleted a chunk of MY COMPOSITION and I wasn't happy about that. She played back the alteration with a demo of the vocal concept and it was perfect. That change is the part where she speaks french then says "shhh" and the music cuts to silence. It was a bold decision to delete a chuck of another composers song but it made for such a nice addition.

Good Job Mae!

This track was also mixed nad mastered by Tim Webberson.
